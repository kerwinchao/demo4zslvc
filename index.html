1. Abstract

Zero-shot voice conversion (VC) is a challenging task, aiming to convert speech produced by a source speaker to that of any speaker even unseen during training. However, existing methods are insufficient to meet this task's requirements in terms of speaker similarity for unseen target speakers, especially out-of-domain (OOD) speakers whose speech has different recording conditions from that of the training corpus. In this work, MTCR-VC is proposed to achieve robust zero-shot VC in both in-domain (ID) and OOD speakers. Specifically, we propose \textit{\textbf{m}ulti-level \textbf{t}emporal-\textbf{c}hannel \textbf{r}etrieval (MTCR)}, which takes advantage of the speaker-discriminative knowledge learned by a speaker verification (SV) model to hierarchically query speaker information in temporal and channel dimensions. In this way, our model can reduce redundant speaker-unrelated information and effectively extract fine-grained speaker representations from the speech of target speakers. To ensure the ability of disentanglement and generalization, a cycle-based training strategy with perceptual constraints is further introduced to guide the disentanglement process and mitigate the mismatch between training and inference. Experiments conducted on both ID and OOD unseen speakers demonstrate that our proposed MTCR-VC achieves new state-of-the-art performance for zero-shot VC in terms of speaker similarity.
